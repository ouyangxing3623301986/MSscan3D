Q&A • Frequently Asked Questions

We have compiled high-frequency questions regarding high-precision scanners and software to help you solve your doubts quickly:

Q1: What are the hardware requirements for the MSscan3D system?

Recommended Specs: Windows 10/11 64-bit, Intel i5 / AMD Ryzen 5 or above, 16GB RAM or above.

GPU Requirements: High-density 3D reconstruction requires floating-point power. NVIDIA discrete GPU (e.g. GTX 1060, RTX 2060/3060/4060 or above, 4GB+ VRAM) is highly recommended. Better GPU means faster reconstructions.

Q2: Why are points missing when scanning black, reflective, or transparent parts? How to solve it?

This is because structured light scanners rely on optical projection and image capturing. Black surfaces absorb pattern light, while reflective or transparent parts distort patterns, causing phase-decoding failure.

Perfect Solution: Uniformly spray a thin layer of 3D scanning developer/spray on the object before scanning. This creates a thin, matte white finish that enables capturing high-density 3D points without affecting dimensional accuracy.

Q3: Can scanned models be imported directly into CAD/reverse engineering software?

Absolutely. After fusing point clouds and meshing, MSscan3D directly exports standard .STL and .OBJ high-density triangle mesh formats.

These formats can be imported seamlessly into popular reverse engineering, CAD, and 3D printing software, including Geomagic Design X, Catia, Solidworks, Creo, and slicers.

Q4: Does the software purchase include 1-on-1 remote setup? What about hardware kits?

Software Only: The software is a standard version with built-in camera drivers. We provide detailed HD videos and user manuals. To maintain the highly competitive price, 1-on-1 remote debugging is not included for standalone software purchases.
